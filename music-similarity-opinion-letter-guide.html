1. What Is a Music Similarity Opinion?

A music similarity opinion (sometimes called a music similarity clearance or musicologist opinion letter) is the final, written outcome of a two-step service:

  1. Confidential analysis —an independent forensic musicologist reviews your work, compares it to reference songs, and privately flags any passages that invite copyright risk, along with practical suggestions for revision.
  2. Positive clearance letter —once the track clears that threshold (more than 85 percent do after minor or no tweaks), you receive a concise opinion letter that confirms the music is unlikely to infringe existing works.

The written letter itself is always affirmative; any sensitive or incriminating analysis is shared verbally and remains confidential.

What the Letter Contains

  • Project overview – a concise description of the cleared track
  • Method summary – reference works considered and elements examined
  • Clearance statement – professional opinion that the track is not substantially similar to protected works
  • Signature & credentials – establishing independence and expertise

Music Similarity Opinion vs. Sample Clearance

“Sample” is often used interchangeably with “copying” or “plagiarism,” but they are distinct concepts. Sample clearance focuses on the sound recording and whether you inserted actual audio from another work. A music similarity opinion focuses on the composition and musical expression even when no audio sampling occurred.

When Do Clients Request One?

  1. Pre-release: before a single or album goes public
  2. Pre-use in media: prior to placing music in advertising, film, or game projects
  3. Sound-alike sanity check: when a track intentionally or unintentionally feels close to a known song

A rapid review either confirms the track is clear or highlights tweaks you can make before publishing. The resulting letter reduces the risk of similarity claims and demonstrates professional due diligence to labels, agencies, and distributors.

In short: the MusicSimilarityCheck service gives you candid, confidential feedback first, then a formal clearance letter once your music is ready for release.
