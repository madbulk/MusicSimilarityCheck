Do I Need a Music Similarity Opinion Before Release?

Before you release or pitch your track, the last thing you want is a costly infringement claim derailing your plans. A music similarity risk opinion gives you expert peace of mind—here’s when and why you need one.

What Is a Music Similarity Risk Opinion?

A music similarity risk opinion is a formal, written analysis by a forensic musicologist. It assesses whether your work is likely to be deemed substantially similar to existing music, reducing your exposure to infringement claims. Unlike automated tools or informal opinions, this is a court-tested, defensible report.

When You Need One

  • Agency & Commercial Work: Advertising and sync agencies often require documented review before green-lighting a track.
  • Label or Public Release: Avoid takedowns or post-release disputes by vetting similarity risk early in the process.
  • Independent Creators: Find peace of mind—even solo artists benefit from an expert second opinion before dropping new music.
