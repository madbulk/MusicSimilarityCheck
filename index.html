What I Do

A musicologist audits your track, optionally against a named reference and evaluates copyright-related risk associated with protectable elements that may echo existing works. Where risky passages are flagged, practical musical fixes are offered to alleviate those risks before release.

Why It Matters

  • Mitigate risk. Fix issues efficiently now, minimizing risks of both legitimate and illegitimate infringement claims.
  • Reduce statutory-damage exposure. A documented good-faith review argues against a claim of willful infringement, which carries exponentiall greater penalties than unwillful.
  • Secure your placement. Agencies and rights teams often require documented review before broadcast. It's become standard risk-mitigation practice.

How It Works

  1. Upload your track (and any reference song of concern).
  2. Receive a secure payment link immediately after submission.
  3. If the work clears, a written musicologist's document (clearance) arrives within the turnaround you select. If concerns are found, musicologist explains and makes change suggestions. Following revisions, the work is resubmitted for review.
